Synopsis

"The Wright Brothers" is a biography written by David McCullough. It delves into the lives of Orville and Wilbur Wright, the inventors credited with creating and flying the world's first successful powered aircraft. McCullough meticulously details the brothers' upbringing, their relentless pursuit of flight, and the challenges they faced in achieving their groundbreaking feat. The book explores their innovative spirit, their dedication to experimentation, and the profound impact their invention had on the course of history.
